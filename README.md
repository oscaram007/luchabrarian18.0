## What Is This Game?

*The Luchabrarian* is a fast-paced action game that runs entirely inside a single web page. There is nothing to download, nothing to install, and no app store required. You open the file in any modern web browser and the game starts immediately.

The premise is a joyful collision of two worlds: the ancient, dignified library and the wild, theatrical world of lucha libre wrestling. You play as a masked luchador librarian whose job is to file books onto the correct shelves before time runs out, all while battling a relentless parade of ghosts who want to stop you.

It is chaotic. It is fast. It is genuinely funny. And underneath the surface, it is a surprisingly deep piece of software.

---

## The Story in Plain Language

A library is under attack. Ghosts of every shape and temperament have invaded the stacks. The books are scattered everywhere. Only one person stands between order and chaos: **El Luchabrarian**, a masked wrestler who takes cataloguing very seriously.

Your job is to run around the library floor, pick up books, and carry them to the matching shelves before the countdown reaches zero. File a Romance novel to the Romance shelf. Drop a Science book on the Science shelf. Throw Trash books into the trash can. Simple enough, until the ghosts show up and everything falls apart in the best possible way.

## How to Play

### The Goal

File as many books as possible before time expires. Every correctly shelved book earns points. Chain multiple correct filings in a row to build a combo multiplier that sends your score soaring. Survive the ghosts. Beat the clock.

### Controls

**On a keyboard:**

| Action | Key |
|---|---|
| Move | WASD or Arrow Keys |
| Dash (also parries!) | Spacebar |
| Throw a book at a ghost | Hold Q (charge for power) |
| Charge Attack | Hold E (costs 3 books) |
| Open the mini-map | M |
| View performance stats | P |
| Pause | Escape |

**On a phone or tablet:**

A virtual joystick appears on the left side of the screen. Three buttons appear on the right: DASH, THROW, and CHARGE.

### The Core Loop

1. Walk over a scattered book to pick it up. You can carry up to 5 at once.
2. Walk to the shelf that matches the book's color and type.
3. Touch the shelf. The book files automatically.
4. Points appear, your combo rises, and particles fly everywhere.
5. Repeat as fast as possible while ghosts try to drain your health.

### Health and Damage

You start with 100 health points. Ghosts deal 20 damage on contact. On Easy difficulty, your health regenerates slowly when no ghosts are nearby. On Hard difficulty, it does not regenerate at all. If your health reaches zero, the run ends.

## The Ghosts

This is where the game reveals its depth. There are **15 distinct ghost types**, each with a different personality, behavior, and strategy. Knowing what each ghost does is the difference between a mediocre run and a legendary one.

### Standard Threats

**Normal Ghost** is your basic pursuer. It moves at a steady pace and chases you directly. Nothing fancy.

**Fast Ghost** is smaller and quicker, leaving a faint motion trail as it rushes toward you. It is the first real threat beginners encounter.

**Tank Ghost** is slow but absorbs significantly more damage. It wears faint armour rings and will not go down easily.

**Teleporter Ghost** warps across the screen unpredictably. It leaves a glitchy chromatic shimmer when it moves.

**Boss Ghost** is the largest, most dangerous ghost in the game. It announces itself with a dramatic banner, wears a golden crown, displays a visible health bar, and hits hard enough to feel it.

### Strategic Threats

**Swarm Ghost** looks like a single enemy but splits into three smaller ghosts when killed. Each of the three mini-ghosts then chases you independently. Killing it carelessly makes things worse.

**Ranged Ghost** keeps its distance and fires magical projectiles at you. A targeting reticle glows before it shoots. You can dash into a projectile with perfect timing to reflect it back.

**Bomber Ghost** rushes directly at you with a lit fuse on its head. The closer it gets, the faster it flashes. When it reaches you, it explodes in a wide radius that also damages nearby ghosts. You can use it against itself.

**Shielder Ghost** carries a rotating arc of energy that blocks attacks from one direction. You must hit it from the unprotected side.

**Chain Ghost** always spawns with a partner. The two are linked by a glowing tether. Damage to one is shared with the other, and they pull each other through space like a two-body problem.

### Specialist Threats

**Possessor Ghost** targets a library shelf and sinks into it. While possessing a shelf, the shelf becomes corrupted: it glows red, changes what book type it accepts, and breaks your filing chain. Kill the ghost to restore the shelf.

**Shusher Ghost** is a slow, bespectacled librarian ghost that emits a wide silence aura. Any power-up you have active becomes suppressed while you stand inside that aura. It forces you to engage or lose your buffs.

**Overdue Ghost** targets a shelf and stamps it with an "OVERDUE" marking, corrupting it for up to 10 seconds if not defeated. The stamp pulses visibly on the ghost's body.

**Mimic Ghost** disguises itself as a book lying on the floor. It holds perfectly still until you wander close, then reveals itself and chases you at high speed. The moment of reveal is intentionally startling.

**Plagiarist Ghost** disguises itself as whatever type of book you most recently picked up. It is stationary while hidden but chases aggressively once spotted.

## Combat Actions

### The Dash and Parry

Pressing Spacebar launches you into a burst of speed. On Easy difficulty, dashing through a ghost deals damage and sends it flying. On all difficulties, dashing at the *exact* moment a projectile reaches you creates a perfect parry: the projectile reverses and flies back at the ghost that fired it. Timing is tight, but the payoff is enormous.

### Book Throwing

Holding Q charges a throw. A quick tap throws with base force. Holding longer builds charge for a heavier, faster projectile that deals more damage and creates a bigger impact on contact. Books interact with ghost weaknesses: certain book types deal double damage to specific ghost varieties.

### Charge Attack

Holding E while carrying at least three books triggers a wide area explosion centered on the player. It consumes all three books and damages every ghost within range simultaneously. It is a last resort and a crowd-clearer.

## Scoring System

Points are calculated at the moment a book is successfully filed.

The base value of each book is 100 points. That value is then multiplied by your current combo count, raised to the power of 1.5, up to a maximum of 10 combo stacks in the formula. On top of that, any active Double Points power-up doubles the result, any Tablets shelf wisdom bonus stacks further, and the shelf-specific bonus multiplier applies last.

A single filing at a high combo, with Double Points active and a Mystery shelf bonus in play, can be worth many thousands of points.

### Combo Announcements

Reaching certain combo thresholds triggers a full-screen announcement with escalating drama:

- 5× combo: NICE!
- 8× combo: GREAT!
- 12× combo: AMAZING!
- 18× combo: LEGENDARY!
- 25× combo: UNSTOPPABLE!
- 35× combo: LIBRARY GOD!

## The Escalation System

Every 20 seconds, the difficulty escalates one level regardless of what you have done. Higher escalation levels increase ghost spawn frequency, increase ghost movement speed, and unlock access to more dangerous ghost varieties in the spawn pool. This ensures no run ever becomes comfortable for long.

A boss wave banner appears every 60 seconds, announcing a forced boss ghost spawn. The screen shakes. The music shifts. Dramatic things follow.

## The Mini-Map

Pressing M toggles an overhead map in the lower right corner of the screen. It shows the positions of all shelves, all ghosts, and the trash can. It also displays small color-coded dots for each shelf type so you can navigate the library quickly when carrying a specific book.

## What Languages and Technologies Power This Game

*The Luchabrarian* is built entirely using three technologies that are native to every modern web browser. No outside frameworks, no game engines, no paid tools.

### HTML (HyperText Markup Language)

HTML is the skeleton of every web page. In this game, the entire HTML structure is intentionally minimal: one `<canvas>` element where the game is drawn, and one `<script>` block where all the logic lives. HTML provides the container. Everything visible is drawn on the canvas programmatically rather than built from HTML elements.

### CSS (Cascading Style Sheets)

CSS controls the visual presentation of the web page itself. In this game, CSS is used for the background gradient behind the canvas, the scanline overlay that gives the screen a retro CRT texture, the canvas sizing rules that stretch it to fill the entire browser window, and the `Press Start 2P` font imported from Google Fonts. CSS does not draw any of the game. It dresses the stage.

### JavaScript

JavaScript is where the entire game lives. Every ghost, every book, every particle, every animation frame, every collision check, every score calculation, and every pixel drawn on screen is produced by JavaScript code. The game is a single JavaScript program running inside the `<script>` tag, approximately 15,000 lines long.

## Key Programming Concepts Used

You do not need to understand code to enjoy the game, but the following concepts shaped how it was built. Each one is explained in plain terms.

### The Game Loop

A game loop is a function that runs continuously, dozens of times per second. Each time it runs, it does two things: it updates the state of every object in the game (positions, health values, timers, animations), and then it draws the current state to the screen. In this game, the loop targets 60 frames per second. The browser provides a built-in function called `requestAnimationFrame` that triggers the loop at the right pace.

### The Canvas API

The `<canvas>` element in HTML is essentially a blank drawing surface. JavaScript can draw shapes, text, gradients, arcs, and images onto it using a set of drawing commands called the Canvas 2D API. Every ghost silhouette, every shelf, every particle, and every HUD element in this game is drawn using those commands, executed fresh every frame. The screen is cleared and redrawn 60 times per second, creating the illusion of movement.

### Delta Time

Every frame, the game measures how many milliseconds have passed since the last frame. This value is called delta time (often shortened to `dt`). All movement, timers, and animations are calculated using delta time rather than frame count. This ensures the game runs at the same speed whether the computer is fast or slow. A ghost that moves 100 pixels per second will cover 100 pixels in one second regardless of whether the computer drew 60 frames or 45 frames in that second.

### Object Pools

Creating thousands of particle objects every second and then discarding them would slow down the game because the browser's memory manager would constantly be cleaning up. The game solves this with an object pool: a collection of pre-created particle objects that are borrowed when needed and returned to the collection when finished, rather than created and destroyed. This keeps memory usage stable and the frame rate smooth.

### Spatial Hashing

Checking whether every ghost has collided with every book and every shelf every frame would require an enormous number of comparisons. The game uses a spatial hash grid to solve this efficiently. The play area is divided into a grid of cells. Each object registers itself in the cell it currently occupies. Collision checks only look at objects in the same cell or adjacent cells, reducing thousands of potential comparisons to a small handful. This is why the game can have many ghosts active simultaneously without slowing down.

### Object-Oriented State Management

The game tracks every entity (player, ghosts, books, shelves, particles, power-ups, projectiles) as a JavaScript object with properties describing its current state. Each frame, the update function reads those properties, applies the physics and game rules, and writes new values. The render function then reads those same properties and draws accordingly. Separating update logic from drawing logic keeps the code organized and predictable.

### Touch Input Normalization

On mobile devices, the game replaces keyboard input with a virtual joystick and three virtual buttons drawn directly on the canvas. The touch events from the screen are translated into the same internal input flags that keyboard events produce. The rest of the game's logic does not need to know whether input came from a keyboard or a touchscreen. It simply reads the flags.

### Procedural Level Generation

The shelf layout is different every run. At the start of each game, the 12 shelf types are shuffled randomly and placed into a 4-column, 3-row grid. Each shelf is then jittered slightly within its cell, pushed apart from any shelf that ended up too close using a repulsion algorithm, and moved away from the player's starting position. The result is a layout that feels organic rather than static.

## Visual Effects

The game uses several layers of visual feedback to make every action feel satisfying.

**Particles** are small objects with their own positions, velocities, gravity, and fade timers. The game supports star sparks, flutter pages, dust clouds, expanding shockwave rings, rising bubbles, firework bursts, floating hearts, and drifting code symbols. Up to 1,200 particles can exist simultaneously.

**Screen shake** is triggered by impacts. Ghost collisions, bomb explosions, and boss deaths shake the camera by offsetting the entire canvas draw position by a random amount that decays over time.

**Hit-stop** is a technique borrowed from fighting games. When a significant impact occurs, the game briefly clamps delta time to near zero, effectively freezing the action for a fraction of a second. This makes hits feel heavier and more impactful without actually pausing the game.

**Camera zoom** pulls the view out slightly when the player reaches a high combo, providing a subtle sense of scale and momentum.

**Ghost silhouettes** are drawn using Bezier curves: a dome arc across the top and three scalloped bumps along the bottom. Each ghost type receives a unique radial gradient fill, a per-type outer glow halo, and expressive eyes designed to communicate personality at a glance.

## The Character: El Luchabrarian

The player character is rendered in full animated detail. The luchador wears a mask whose colors shift based on the selected difficulty: green and gold for Easy, red and gold for Medium, black and red for Hard. The character has a walk cycle with four frames of animation, an idle animation with subtle weight shift, and a cape that swings during movement. A dash trail of motion blur copies follows the player during a dash.

## What Makes This a Learning Tool

A teacher presenting this game to students has access to a wide range of discussion threads embedded in its design.

The game demonstrates **how web technologies work together** in a concrete and visible way. HTML creates the frame. CSS styles the presentation. JavaScript runs the logic. Students can open the browser's developer tools, inspect the single file, and see all three technologies in one place.

It demonstrates **why games need efficient code**. The difference between checking every ghost against every object (slow) and using spatial hashing (fast) is not abstract. You can watch it in the performance overlay by pressing P during gameplay.

It demonstrates **how randomness is used thoughtfully**. The shelf layouts change every run, but the repulsion algorithm ensures no two shelves are ever unreachably close. The ghost spawn pool changes based on the wave number. Randomness is constrained, not unlimited.

It demonstrates **the game loop as a concept**, which connects to any real-time system: animation, simulation, music visualization, or interactive data.

It demonstrates **responsive design**, because the game recalculates every position and control layout whenever the browser window is resized, including replacing the entire input model when a touch device is detected.

And it demonstrates **the power of creative constraints**. The entire game is a single HTML file. One file. No libraries. No build tools. No dependencies. Just the three languages that have always lived in every browser, used with genuine craft.

## File Structure

The game is distributed as a single file: `index.html`.

That file contains three sections in order:

1. A comment block at the top with copyright information.
2. A `<style>` block with the CSS rules for the page and background.
3. A `<script>` block containing the entire game engine, approximately 15,000 lines of JavaScript.

There are no external image files. No audio files. No separate JavaScript files. No CSS files. No folders. Every visual element is drawn programmatically. Every font is loaded from Google Fonts at runtime. Every sound, if any, is synthesized by the browser. The entire game is self-contained in that one file.

## Glossary for Non-Technical Readers

**Canvas** — A drawing surface provided by the browser. Think of it as a whiteboard that JavaScript can draw on and erase 60 times per second.

**Frame** — One complete draw of the screen. A game running at 60 frames per second redraws the screen 60 times every second.

**Game loop** — The heartbeat of the game. A function that repeats endlessly, updating positions and drawing everything fresh each time it runs.

**Object** — In programming, an object is a bundle of related data. A ghost object might contain its position, health, speed, and type all in one place.

**Particle** — A small visual element with a short lifespan, used for sparks, smoke, page flutters, and explosions. Hundreds run simultaneously.

**Delta time** — The time between frames, used to make movement consistent regardless of computer speed.

**Hitbox** — The invisible area around an object used to detect collisions. In this game, most hitboxes are circles.

**Combo** — A chain of successful actions performed without a break. Here, filing books without being hit or letting too much time pass between filings.

**Escalation** — The automatic, timed increase in difficulty as the run progresses.

**Spatial hash** — A technique that divides space into a grid to speed up collision detection by only checking nearby objects.

**Object pool** — A recycling system for short-lived objects like particles, preventing memory waste.

**Procedural** — Generated by an algorithm rather than placed manually. Each run's shelf layout is procedural.
